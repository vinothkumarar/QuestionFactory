# PART 7 — DIFFICULTY CALIBRATION

## Objective

Difficulty shall be determined by conceptual complexity rather than
calculation length.

A difficult question is not one that requires lengthy arithmetic.

A difficult question requires deeper understanding, reasoning,
and application of concepts.

---

## Difficulty Levels

Question Factory OS supports five difficulty levels.

### Level 1 — Foundation

Characteristics:

- Single concept
- Direct application
- Minimal reasoning
- Suitable for beginners

Target Success Rate:
80–95%

---

### Level 2 — Easy+

Characteristics:

- One or two connected ideas
- Moderate reasoning
- Standard examination style

Target Success Rate:
60–80%

---

### Level 3 — Medium

Characteristics:

- Multiple conceptual steps
- Careful analysis required
- Common JEE Main standard

Target Success Rate:
40–60%

---

### Level 4 — Hard

Characteristics:

- Multi-concept integration
- Advanced reasoning
- Non-obvious solution path

Target Success Rate:
20–40%

---

### Level 5 — Elite

Characteristics:

- Deep conceptual insight
- Original thinking
- Advanced JEE level
- High discrimination power

Target Success Rate:
Below 20%

---

## Difficulty Principles

Difficulty should increase through:

- Conceptual depth
- Logical reasoning
- Multi-step thinking
- Integration of concepts

Difficulty should NOT increase through:

- Excessive calculations
- Long numerical substitutions
- Confusing wording
- Artificial tricks
- Hidden assumptions

---

## Calibration Rules

When generating questions:

- Match the requested difficulty level.
- Ensure consistency between question, solution and explanation.
- Maintain fairness.
- Avoid accidental difficulty inflation.

---

## Final Verification

Before returning the question verify:

- Difficulty matches the requested level.
- Reasoning complexity is appropriate.
- Question length is justified.
- Solution path is educational.
