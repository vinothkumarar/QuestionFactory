# PART 10 — FINAL ASSEMBLY

## Objective

The Question Generation Specification (QGS) shall be assembled from
modular specification files.

The generated specification represents the complete instruction set
provided to the AI model.

---

## Assembly Order

The final QGS shall be assembled in the following sequence.

1. System Identity

2. Mission

3. Educational Philosophy

4. JEE Examination Standards

5. Blueprint Rules

6. Distractor Engineering

7. Difficulty Calibration

8. Output Contract

9. Self Validation

10. Question Template

---

## Template Injection

After assembling the QGS, append the selected Question Template.

Examples:

- JEE MCQ Template
- Integer Answer Template
- Assertion Reason Template
- Multiple Correct Template
- Matrix Match Template

The template defines the task.

The QGS defines the quality standards.

---

## Provider Independence

The assembled specification shall remain independent of the AI provider.

Supported providers include:

- OpenAI
- Gemini
- Claude
- Ollama
- Future compatible Large Language Models

No provider-specific instructions shall appear inside the QGS.

---

## Execution Philosophy

Question Factory OS follows the pipeline:

Blueprint

↓

Question Generation Specification (QGS)

↓

Prompt Builder

↓

AI Provider

↓

Response Parser

↓

Validation Engine

↓

Repair Engine (Future)

↓

Approved Question

---

## Educational Authority

The Frozen Blueprint remains the educational source of truth.

The QGS is the executable educational specification derived from
the Frozen Blueprint.

Whenever future revisions occur:

Blueprint
        ↓
QGS
        ↓
Prompt
        ↓
Generated Question

Only the Blueprint and QGS should require modification.

The Engine architecture should remain stable.

---

## Versioning

Every QGS release shall be versioned.

Examples:

QGS v1.0

QGS v1.1

QGS v2.0

Changes shall be documented before production use.

---

## Long-Term Vision

Question Factory OS shall evolve into a provider-independent,
exam-independent assessment generation platform.

Future examination support includes:

- IIT JEE Main
- IIT JEE Advanced
- NEET
- BITSAT
- Olympiads
- CBSE
- State Boards
- Institution-specific assessments

The engine architecture shall remain unchanged.

Only:

- Metadata
- QGS
- Prompt Templates

shall evolve.
