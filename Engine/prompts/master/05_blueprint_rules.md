# PART 5 — QUESTION FACTORY BLUEPRINT RULES

## Fundamental Principle

Every generated question shall comply with the Question Factory OS
Frozen Blueprint.

The Blueprint is the educational source of truth.

Whenever there is a conflict between general AI behavior and this
Blueprint, the Blueprint shall take precedence.

---

## R01 — Concept First

Generate questions that evaluate conceptual understanding rather
than memorization.

The concept being tested must be clearly identifiable.

---

## R02 — Single Learning Objective

Every question should primarily assess one well-defined learning
objective.

Avoid mixing unrelated concepts unless explicitly required.

---

## R03 — Mathematical Verification

Before returning the final answer, internally verify:

- Formulae
- Calculations
- Units
- Dimensions
- Logical consistency

Never return unverified mathematics.

---

## R04 — Unique Answer

Unless the question type specifies otherwise:

- Exactly one option shall be correct.
- No option shall be partially correct.
- No ambiguity shall exist.

---

## R05 — Explanation Quality

Every explanation must:

- justify the correct answer,
- explain the governing concept,
- be educational,
- be logically complete.

---

## R06 — Difficulty Alignment

Difficulty shall match the requested level.

Do not artificially increase difficulty through lengthy calculations.

Difficulty must arise from conceptual depth.

---

## R07 — Language Quality

Use:

- clear English
- precise terminology
- concise wording
- professional examination style

Avoid conversational language.

---

## R08 — Examination Authenticity

Questions should resemble official examination papers.

Avoid patterns that clearly reveal the answer.

---

## R09 — Originality

Generate original questions.

Questions may be inspired by historical examination trends but
shall not directly reproduce copyrighted examination questions.

---

## R10 — Educational Value

Every question should improve learning.

The explanation should teach, not merely reveal the answer.

---

## R11 — Internal Consistency

Verify consistency between:

- question
- options
- answer
- explanation

Every component must agree with every other component.

---

## R12 — Production Readiness

Before returning the final output verify that the question is suitable
for direct inclusion in a production examination database.
