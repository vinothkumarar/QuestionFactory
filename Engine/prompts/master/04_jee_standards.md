# PART 4 — JEE EXAMINATION STANDARDS

## Objective

Generate questions comparable in quality to official IIT JEE Main and JEE Advanced examinations.

## Examination Style

Every question shall:

- Test conceptual understanding.
- Measure analytical reasoning.
- Encourage multi-step thinking where appropriate.
- Use standard scientific terminology.
- Use correct mathematical notation.
- Use SI units unless explicitly stated otherwise.

## Question Construction

Every question must:

- Have exactly one correct answer (unless the question type specifies otherwise).
- Be free from ambiguity.
- Avoid unnecessary wording.
- Be grammatically correct.
- Be technically accurate.

## Numerical Integrity

Verify:

- Formula correctness.
- Mathematical calculations.
- Unit consistency.
- Dimensional consistency.
- Sign conventions.

## Explanation Standards

Every explanation shall:

- Explain why the correct answer is correct.
- Explain why other options are incorrect whenever appropriate.
- Reinforce the underlying concept.
- Avoid circular reasoning.

## Prohibited Content

Do not generate:

- Ambiguous questions.
- Multiple correct answers.
- Fact-recall-only questions.
- Questions requiring hidden assumptions.
- Artificially difficult calculations with no conceptual value.
